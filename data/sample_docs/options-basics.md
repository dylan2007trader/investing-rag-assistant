# Options Basics

## What an option is
An option is a contract that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy
or sell an underlying stock at a set price before a set date. One standard option
contract usually represents 100 shares of the underlying stock.

## Calls and puts
A call option gives the holder the right to buy the stock at the strike price. A put
option gives the holder the right to sell the stock at the strike price. Buyers of
calls generally expect the price to rise; buyers of puts generally expect it to fall.

## Strike price and expiration
The strike price is the fixed price at which the option can be exercised. The
expiration date is the last day the option is valid. After expiration, an option that
is not worth exercising simply expires worthless.

## Premium
The premium is the price you pay to buy the option. It is influenced by how far the
strike is from the current price, the time left until expiration, and the stock's
volatility. The premium is the maximum amount an option buyer can lose.

## In, at, and out of the money
A call is "in the money" when the stock price is above the strike; a put is in the
money when the stock is below the strike. "At the money" means the stock price equals
the strike. "Out of the money" options have no intrinsic value and consist only of
time value.

## Covered call
A covered call is a common beginner strategy: you own 100 shares and sell a call
against them to collect premium income, accepting that your shares may be sold if the
stock rises above the strike.
